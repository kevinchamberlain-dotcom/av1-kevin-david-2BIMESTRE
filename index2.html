About KLM's Mainline fleet

Mainline fleet

Narrowbody fleet

KLM Royal Dutch Airlines operates a fleet of around 125 aircraft according to Wikipedia. The Airbus A321neo was first introduced into the Dutch Flag carrier's fleet in August 2024. These aircraft alongside the smaller Airbus A320neo, which will also join the fleet in 2028, will replace the carrier´s Boeing 737 aircraft used for short haul services. The Embraer 195-E2 at regional carrier KLM Cityhopper will replace the airline´s older Embraer 190s.

The Airbus A320neo family is 50% quieter and burns 25% less fuel than previous aircraft. These improvements in fleet renewal will significantly reduce CO2 emmisions and environmental inpact.
