Mainline fleet

Narrowbody fleet

KLM Royal Dutch Airlines operates a fleet of around 125 aircraft according to Wikipedia. The Airbus A321neo was first introduced into the Dutch Flag carrier's fleet in August 2024. These aircraft alongside the smaller Airbus A320neo, which will also join the fleet in 2028, will replace the carrier´s Boeing 737 aircraft used for short haul services. The Embraer 195-E2 at regional carrier KLM Cityhopper will replace the airline´s older Embraer 190s.

The Airbus A320neo family is 50% quieter and burns 25% less fuel than previous aircraft. These improvements in fleet renewal will significantly reduce CO2 emmisions and environmental inpact. These also improving passenger experience and comfort.

Widebody fleet

On the other side of the house, the airline also operates long haul services with both Airbus and Boeing types. These types include the Airbus A330 ,Boeing 777 and Boeing 787 Dreamliner aircraft to various destinations across the globe like North America, South America, Africa and Asia. The carrier will also introduce the Airbus A350 900 in 2026 to replace its older Airbus A330s and Boeing 777s.
